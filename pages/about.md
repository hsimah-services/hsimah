G'day! I'm Hamish Blake - a software engineer at Meta, Australian expat, and transplant Seattleite.

## What I Do

I work on the Ent Framework in Meta's Core Data org. My bread and butter is scalable backend systems - the kind where data integrity isn't optional and you're thinking about what the schema looks like in five years, not five sprints. That said, I genuinely enjoy putting together a clean, efficient UI. There's something satisfying about a well-laid-out interface that just *works*.

I've been doing this for over 15 years now. It started, as many great careers do, with messing around in mIRC. I wrote scripts in mSL to make my IRC experience cooler, discovered I liked making computers do things, and somehow fell into a software engineering degree. The rest is history.

## Outside the Terminal

When I'm not at a keyboard, you'll find me on one of my bikes (yes, I have four - no, I don't think that's too many). I'm also a big reader and I collect vinyl, because apparently I enjoy hobbies that take up physical space. I also collect felt hats - so far I've picked them up from Australia, Utah, Wyoming, and Tennessee. If you know a good hatmaker, I'm always in the market.

On top of all that, I'm currently learning both Spanish and French, and doing neither particularly well.

I have two pomskies, Laiko and Belki, who are the real stars of this household. They are spoiled beyond reason and I have zero regrets about it.

## This Blog

This is my technical blog - expect write-ups on engineering problems, system design ethos, and the occasional deep dive into whatever rabbit hole I've fallen into. The tone here is casual; think of it as a conversation between nerds. If you're after something more polished, you're in the wrong place.

## This Site

Built with vanilla Web Components, TypeScript, and Vite. No frameworks, no runtime dependencies beyond `marked` for markdown rendering. Just the platform.
